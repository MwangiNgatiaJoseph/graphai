# GraphAI Forex Trading Analysis Guide

## Overview

GraphAI is fully optimized for analyzing forex trading charts from any trading platform. Whether you're using MetaTrader 4, MetaTrader 5, TradingView, or any other forex app, you can upload your charts and get AI-powered analysis instantly.

## Supported Chart Types

### Candlestick Charts
The most common forex chart format. GraphAI analyzes:
- **Candlestick patterns** (doji, engulfing, hammer, shooting star, etc.)
- **Open, high, low, close (OHLC) values**
- **Wicks and body relationships** for trend signals
- **Support and resistance levels** formed by candlestick positions

### Line Charts
Simplified price movement visualization. GraphAI identifies:
- **Trend direction** (uptrend, downtrend, sideways)
- **Price levels** and key breakout points
- **Moving averages** overlaid on the chart
- **Volatility patterns** from line slope

### Bar Charts
Alternative OHLC representation. GraphAI analyzes:
- **Price range** (high-low spread)
- **Opening and closing positions** relative to the range
- **Volume implications** from bar height
- **Trend confirmation** from consecutive bars

## Technical Indicators Recognized

GraphAI can identify and explain these common forex indicators:

| Indicator | What It Shows | Forex Application |
|-----------|---------------|-------------------|
| **Moving Averages (MA)** | Average price over time | Trend identification, support/resistance |
| **RSI (Relative Strength Index)** | Momentum (0-100 scale) | Overbought (>70) / Oversold (<30) signals |
| **MACD** | Momentum and trend | Crossovers signal entry/exit points |
| **Bollinger Bands** | Volatility and price range | Breakout and mean reversion trades |
| **Stochastic Oscillator** | Momentum in range (0-100) | Overbought/oversold conditions |
| **ATR (Average True Range)** | Volatility measure | Position sizing and stop-loss placement |
| **Volume** | Trading activity | Trend confirmation or reversal warning |
| **Fibonacci Levels** | Support/resistance zones | Retracement and extension targets |

## How to Use GraphAI for Forex Trading

### Step 1: Capture Your Chart
Take a screenshot of your forex chart from your trading platform. Ensure the chart clearly shows:
- Currency pair (e.g., EUR/USD, GBP/JPY)
- Timeframe (1M, 5M, 15M, 1H, 4H, 1D, 1W, 1M)
- Price levels and current price
- Any technical indicators you're using

### Step 2: Upload to GraphAI
1. Click **"Start Analyzing"** on the GraphAI home page
2. Enter a title (e.g., "EUR/USD 4H Chart - June 29")
3. Add optional context (e.g., "Looking for entry signals")
4. Upload your chart image via drag-and-drop or click-to-upload
5. Click **"Start Analysis"**

### Step 3: Ask Trading Questions
Once your chart is uploaded, ask GraphAI specific questions:

**Trend Analysis:**
- "What is the current trend direction?"
- "Where are the support and resistance levels?"
- "Is this an uptrend or downtrend?"

**Technical Indicators:**
- "What does the RSI indicate right now?"
- "Are we in overbought or oversold territory?"
- "What do the moving averages suggest?"

**Trading Signals:**
- "What are potential entry points?"
- "Where should I place my stop-loss?"
- "What is the risk-reward ratio for this setup?"

**Pattern Recognition:**
- "What candlestick patterns do you see?"
- "Is there a head and shoulders pattern forming?"
- "What does this breakout suggest?"

**Multi-timeframe Analysis:**
- "Compare this to the daily chart trend"
- "Is this aligned with the weekly trend?"
- "What confluence signals do you see?"

### Step 4: Follow-up Questions
GraphAI maintains full conversation context, so you can ask follow-up questions without re-uploading:
- "What's your confidence level on this signal?"
- "What could invalidate this setup?"
- "What's the next resistance level if we break above?"

Upload charts from different timeframes (1H, 4H, 1D) to confirm signals across timeframes. Confluence across timeframes increases signal reliability.

## Technical Indicator Explanations

### RSI (Relative Strength Index)
- **Range:** 0-100
- **Overbought:** Above 70 (potential reversal)
- **Oversold:** Below 30 (potential reversal)
- **Forex Use:** Identify exhaustion in trends, confirm reversals

### MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence)
- **Components:** MACD line, Signal line, Histogram
- **Bullish Signal:** MACD crosses above signal line
- **Bearish Signal:** MACD crosses below signal line
- **Forex Use:** Confirm trend changes, identify momentum shifts

### Bollinger Bands
- **Components:** 20-period MA with ±2 standard deviations
- **Squeeze:** Low volatility, potential breakout coming
- **Expansion:** High volatility, strong trend
- **Forex Use:** Identify volatility, find support/resistance, spot breakouts

### Moving Averages
- **Fast MA (5, 10, 20):** Short-term trends
- **Slow MA (50, 100, 200):** Long-term trends
- **Golden Cross:** 50 MA crosses above 200 MA (bullish)
- **Death Cross:** 50 MA crosses below 200 MA (bearish)
- **Forex Use:** Confirm trend direction, identify support/resistance
